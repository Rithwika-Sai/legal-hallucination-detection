The judgment is about an appeal made by displaced persons from West Pakistan who were granted quasi permanent allotment of land in the village of Raikot in Ludhiana District in 1949. The father of the appellants, Sardar Nand Singh, was also given quasi permanent allotment of land in another village named Humbran in the same district. The appellants made an application for consolidation of their lands with those of their father, but the Assistant Custodian rejected it on the ground that no land was available in the village Raikot. The application was continued by the appellants after the father's death, but it was rejected again by the Assistant Custodian. The appellants then preferred a revision application before the Custodian General, but it was dismissed by the Additional Custodian. The Additional Custodian also cancelled the allotment of land in the village Karodian to fourteen displaced persons on the ground that they were entitled to allotment of suburban land. The Additional Custodian then made an order cancelling the allotment of land in the village Raikot and instead allotting it to the appellants in Karodian. The land allotted was out of the land released upon the cancellation of allotment of lands in favour of the fourteen displaced persons. The appellants then preferred an application for review of the order cancelling their allotment of land in Raikot.

The parties involved in this case are the appellants, who are displaced persons from West Pakistan, and the respondent No. 1, who is the Assistant Custodian. The legal issue at hand is whether the appellants were entitled to the land they were granted in Raikot.
