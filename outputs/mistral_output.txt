The appellants, displaced persons from West Pakistan, were granted quasi-permanent allotment of lands in village Raikot in 1949. On October 31, 1952, the Additional Custodian cancelled the allotment of fourteen allottees in village Karodian, and also cancelled the appellants' allotment in Raikot but allotted lands to them in Karodian, allotting the Raikot lands to other persons. The fourteen Karodian allottees as well as the appellants applied for review of the orders cancelling their allotments. The Deputy Custodian General restored the Karodian lands to the fourteen allottees on December 17, 1954, whereupon the appellants' Karodian allotment automatically stood cancelled. On January 6, 1955, the appellants moved the Custodian General for calling up their review application and for revising the order of October 31, 1952, cancelling their Raikot allotment. The Custodian General dismissed the application on the ground that his power to revise had been taken away by the Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation) Act, 1954 and the notification issued thereunder on March 24, 1955.
Held, that after the enactment of the 1954 Act, the Custodian General ceased to have the power to cancel allotments. By the issuing of a notification under section 12(1) of that Act, the right, title or interest of the evacuee in the property specified in the notification was extinguished and the property vested absolutely in the Central Government. The right of the Custodian to manage the property under the 1950 Act came to an end and management vested in a new set of officers. Even though no managing officer was appointed or managing corporation constituted under the new Act to manage the property, no one else could exercise the power of cancellation of allotment. The appellants were not entitled to the protection of section 10 of the 1954 Act as they had lost possession of the Raikot lands before the publication of the notification. Bal Mukund vs The State of Punjab, I.L.R. 1957 Punj. 712, approved.
