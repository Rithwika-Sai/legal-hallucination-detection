Parties Involved: Displaced persons from West Pakistan (Appellants) vs. Custodian General of Evacuee Property (Respondent).

Background: The appellants were granted a quasi-permanent land allotment in village Raikot in 1949. On October 31, 1952, the Additional Custodian cancelled their Raikot allotment, shifting them to village Karodian while giving the Raikot lands to other individuals.

The Dispute: Following a separate legal dispute, the original fourteen allottees of Karodian had their lands restored on December 17, 1954, which automatically cancelled the appellants' newly substituted Karodian allotment. The appellants subsequently moved the Custodian General to revive their review application and restore their original Raikot lands.

Core Legal Question: Did the Custodian General retain the legal jurisdiction to revise or cancel land allotments after the enforcement of the Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation) Act, 1954 and its statutory March 24, 1955 notification?

Supreme Court Holding: The appeal was dismissed. The Court held that following the enactment of the 1954 Act, the Custodian General ceased to have the power to alter allotments. By issuing a notification under section 12(1) of that Act, the right, title, or interest of the evacuee in the specified property was extinguished, vesting it absolutely in the Central Government. Consequently, the Custodian’s management rights under the 1950 Act came to an end. Even if no managing officer or corporation had been formally appointed yet under the new Act, no other authority could exercise the power of cancellation. Furthermore, the appellants were not entitled to the protection of section 10 because they had already lost physical possession of the Raikot lands before the notification was published.
